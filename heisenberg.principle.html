Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

A Fundamental Principle of Quantum Mechanics

What is the Uncertainty Principle?

The Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle, formulated by Werner Heisenberg in 1927, states that we cannot simultaneously know both the position and momentum of a quantum particle with arbitrary precision.

ΔxΔp ≥ ℏ/2

Where Δx is the uncertainty in position, Δp is the uncertainty in momentum, and ℏ is the reduced Planck constant

Key Implications

Measurement Impact

The act of measuring a quantum system inevitably disturbs it.

Quantum Limitations

There's a fundamental limit to the precision of our measurements.

Wave-Particle Duality

Supports the wave-particle dual nature of quantum objects.

Scientific Impact

Revolutionized our understanding of quantum mechanics.
